Toast Is Great, But It’s Only Half the Picture.

Toast is a really good register. It tells you what you sold yesterday and which items moved. What it can’t tell you is why people came in, why they didn’t, or what would help tomorrow. That’s where the rest of your data comes in, and where most owners get stuck.

My Framework, Built Over 20+ Years

My 10-Asset Framework
Every Asset Feeds One Dashboard.

This is my plan. Every restaurant has 10 places where data and demand live. Most owners run them like 10 separate apps. I connect all 10 into one view. Each one tells the dashboard what to track, and the dashboard tells the team what to do next.

1

POS (Toast)

The truth layer

Every transaction lives here. We pull daily sales by daypart, top sellers, channel mix (dine-in, takeout, direct, DoorDash, Uber, Grubhub), AOV, and email captures. The dashboard turns this into next-day forecasts, slow-daypart alerts, and triggers that fire SMS, ads, or menu reviews.

2

Website (Search Console + GA4)

The visibility layer

Tells us who finds you, what they search, and what they do once they land. Tracks rankings, click-through, conversions (orders, calls, directions, catering forms), and indexing health. Spots low-CTR pages, broken indexing, and new keyword opportunities.

3

GMB + TripAdvisor + Bing Local

The local discovery layer

The three search engines that move local diners. We pull direction requests, calls, profile views, popular times, top queries, photos, Q&A, and reviews. The dashboard turns review pings into a response queue (1-2 star = today, 3 star = 48 hours, 4-5 star = within 5 days) and flags missed search demand like "catering near me."

4

NAP Reconciliation

The data integrity layer

Your name, address, and phone listed across 30+ directories. Nobody thinks about it until it's killing your rankings. We lock in one official record, fix mismatches, kill duplicates, and add the schema markup on your website so Google trusts you.

5

Social + Nextdoor

The awareness layer

Where you build brand and reach people who aren't searching yet. We track post performance, top content, and Nextdoor reach by neighborhood. We don't shoot your videos. We coach you on what to film using the patterns that already win in food search:

  • "my review of [place]"
  • "i tried [food/place]"
  • "honest review of [place]"
  • "is [place] worth the hype"
  • "ranking every [thing] at [place]"
  • "what i ate at [place]"
  • "first time trying [food]"
  • "[place] vs [place]"
  • "the greatest [food] in [area]"
  • "underrated [place] in [area]"
  • "[area] hidden gem"
  • "must-try [food] in [area]"
  • "stop scrolling if you live in [area]"

One piece of content gets distributed: TikTok, Reels, Facebook Reels, YouTube Shorts, GMB posts, Nextdoor (selective), email newsletter, Meta ads (if it performs).

6

Email

The owned audience layer

Your highest-margin channel. Every send costs almost zero. We track list size, opens, clicks, and revenue per send. Automations running in the background:

  • Welcome series (3 emails over 14 days)
  • Post-purchase thank-you + review request
  • Birthday week free dessert
  • 60-day win-back ($5 off)
  • 90-day deep win-back ($10 off + free side)
  • Monthly VIP offer for top 10% spenders
  • Catering follow-up 90 days after every event
7

Rewards Platform

The retention layer

Turns one-time customers into regulars. Built on Toast Loyalty as the foundation, optionally layered with Marsello, Thanx, or Spendgo. Tracks member AOV vs non-member, visit frequency, point redemptions, and inactive members so the dashboard can fire win-back triggers.

8

Food Apps (DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub)

The third-party demand layer

High volume, high cost. Needs active management. We compare sales, AOV, conversion, and sponsored listing ROAS across platforms, then reallocate spend to whatever's winning. Underperforming items get photo refreshes, rewrites, or get killed. Every order goes out with an insert pushing the customer to your direct ordering link.

9

Reviews Intel Dashboard

The voice-of-customer layer

All reviews from Google, Yelp, TripAdvisor, DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub, Facebook, Nextdoor, and Toast in one inbox (we use Birdeye, Podium, Reputation, or a simple sheet if budget is tight). Each review gets auto-tagged by theme and sentiment. The dashboard flags patterns: if "wait time" shows up 4 times in a week, you get a throughput audit. If "yellow sauce" gets praised 8 times, that becomes the next TikTok, GMB post, and ad.

10

Happy Hour

The customer appreciation layer

A customer appreciation event run during your slow times. Picks up the hours and days the dashboard shows are quiet, and turns them into reasons people choose you. Promoted through Eventbrite, Facebook Events, Luma, GMB Posts, Nextdoor, your email and SMS list, Instagram and TikTok teasers, your /events page, and in-store signage. The dashboard tracks RSVPs, attendance, repeat visits, and email captures per event so we know which formats are worth running again.

On the call, I'll walk through your version of these 10 assets, which ones are working, which are leaking, and which 4 or 5 will move the needle for Chicho's first.
